Silence on the Wire: a Field Guide to Passive Reconnaissance and Indirect Attacks

By Michal Zalewski; Another introductory computer security book, but more in depth than Schneiner's Secrets and Lies. It takes a look at how the computer works, and how it becomes insecure almost practically by design. So far, I'm impressed by what I see.

Hacking: The Art of Exploitation 2nd Edition

By Jon Erickson; A primer book on computer exploitation- like a tutorial on hacking. It has in depth reasons for why these hacks work and how the user can protect himself against it. Provides and adequate background in the early chapters of the book to make it easier for the reader to understand what will come later
